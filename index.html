PROJECT ATLANTIS

Preparing Citizens with Flood Risk Information

Finalists of Aquahacking BC 2020

Canada has the world’s largest coastline and a vast system of rivers. This makes the risk from flooding a serious threat to Canadian communities.

Key Challenges:

1. Most flood maps are older, in the format of stand-alone print maps that are difficult to access and view online.

2. These flood maps are highly technical and difficult to interpret, making them effectively inaccessible to the public.

3. There are significant gaps in current data and flood modelling.

Our Project

Information and Knowledge

Our solution integrates existing flood risk data and maps onto an online platform. This allows us to make the maps more simpler, visually appealing and interactive. We will create intuitive 3D visualizations of flood extent and depth, so that residents can see the impacts of floods on their community. These tools can assist with planning infrastructure, community engagement, and emergency response. We are currently developing an API that allows location-based queries of flood risk.

Communication and Language

On this system, we will communicate flood risk in language that is friendly to the public. Traditionally, annual exceedance probabilities (AEP) have been used for technical evaluations. The current language describes flood risk as the number of years in which it is probable for a given flood event severity to happen. For example, a severe flood may be described as a 100-year flood. However, this language can lead to the misunderstanding that an event of this intensity only happens once in every 100 years. In fact, the likelihood of such an event happening within ten years is 10% and it is entirely possible to have multiple 100-year events happen back-to-back. The current language used to communicate flood risk is not suitable for the public, and thus we will describe flood events in categories, similar to storm events. In combination with our platform, we can show what impact each category of storm causes for a given location.

Data Collection and Modelling

Currently, there are considerable gaps in data and flood modelling. While there have been advancements in surveying technology and flood modelling methods, some communities still cannot afford to produce flood maps. We will provide cost-effective methods for data collection using consumer-grade hardware. We are developing a flood modelling platform based on the cloud, to make it easier for people anywhere to run these models. Together, we will provide the tools for communities to survey their own lands and provide low-cost services.
